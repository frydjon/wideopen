In 2021 I sent a brief email to different artists about going on tour with them & doing a photobook.

<!--endSnippet-->

"Hi Marisa, 
My name is Jonathan - I'm a Miami based photographer and I’m reaching out to you as I’m looking to do a photo book for Noah Kahan."

I only signed on for the first half of the tour. And instead of doing a photobook, I was brought on to do content creation. We did pictures & videos but Noah had a song at the time that wasn't out that he loved, his fans loved & I loved -- Stick Season. So I made a few videos over the course of the few weeks specifically using that song. That song turned out to garner over a billion views & lead him to a Grammy nomination.

For the photos and videos, I intentionally focused on capturing the raw, intimate moments behind the scenes rather than just the high-energy live performances. I wanted to document the quieter, in-between moments—Noah in transit, rehearsing because those were the moments where his artistry felt the most personal and honest.

Visually, I leaned into a more dramatic use of lighting both on an off stage& strayed away from B&W and towards color. The logic here was that I wanted to use a visual language that mirrored the emotional weight of his music—personal yet expansive.
