In 2024 we reached out to ArchivePDF about doing a book for them. At the same time, we were in talks with Offbrand Library about doing a PFW event for them. We realized that both things could coexist & in fact that we could expand the event to a larger concept -- a pop up book store.

<!--endSnippet-->

There's quite a bit here about the story behind how we put this together, it came together very quickly. We saw the opportunity through these two collaborators to create something bigger that encapsulated Wide Open more fully. That's when we came up with the ideas of postcards, it was a really simple way of giving 100+ artists the opportunity to participate in this event.

The goal with the postcards was twofold, to create a large community of artists that represented something more organic than typical Paris Fashion week events because we knew it would generate more attention & give younger, rising artists a bigger platform.

We found a unique spot, it was a wine seller that had been open to the idea of using his storefront for a pop-up. We were able to raise money through Zora and off we went. The goal was to sell archival & independently produced books, magazines & posters. We wanted something that felt grassroots & classy.

A lot of the production help came from @dirt_twerk & @riskflix. There was a lot we learned in doing the pop-up, it probably merits its own blog.
