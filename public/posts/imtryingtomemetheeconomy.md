Lately, I've been thinking about how economic standing isn't built on tangible value anymore. Instead, it's dictated by attention—positive or negative—and the power that perception holds. The first verbalization of this phenomenon came from when I was talking to my gf and told her "I'm tryna meme the economy" there's this deep truth to it. I'm sure some academic writer could point to stats about how much the economy, specifically in America has become detached from the actual state of the economy. Today, you can create wealth and status not through production or labor but through how much attention you command. The image of a thing dictates its reality. Control perception, and you control everything. There's a Beaudrillan facet to all of this.

<!--endSnippet-->

Take influencers vs movie stars for example. Becoming famous is much easier & what that fame produces—culturally or materially—has significantly lowered. These two things are related, the easier it is to produce something, the less time & effort you will decide to spend on it.

Even just 15 years ago, there existed a barrier of entry where, to become prominent in the media landscape you had to exist & build within an existing framework. Even just a few years ago the new class of celebrities - those that built fame online rather than through traditional media - were seen as vapid. While they may still be seen that way they've also been absorbed by traditional media. This is what Kim Kardashian understood so well, she didn't have to create anything meaningful, she just had to generate attention regularly. Fame begets fame. TikTok stars appear in Hollywood productions, and mainstream institutions build entire organizations dedicated to co-opt viral moments. The Hawk Tuah girl was quickly institutionalized (good for her, get your bag).

Or consider this, 20 years ago to be a successful musician, you had to be scouted and signed by a label. They decided your career & could manufacture fame, hits & money for you. 8 years ago during the Soundcloud movement it became clear there was this real gap between the underground & traditional music labels. Young, independent artists could rise to fame by publishing their music online & then using social media to promote it. A lot of the music from that time was hit or miss (I liked alot of it, it's what I grew up on) but they undeniably used shock & controvery to build hype online. And whether you liked it or not, it was successful & authentic, it really was rebellious teens doing anything for attention to get rich (the 21st century American dream). But now, that has been co-opted. What once felt raw and rebellious—kids making chaotic, low-budget music on SoundCloud—has now been packaged and artificially engineered by labels to manufacture virality. Labels largely control social media & falsely generate attention through scripted controvery & TikTok trends. What happened to authentic brainrot?

There's a slew more examples where traditional establishments have been replaced with new models more accessible entry points and where the value a 'thing' is totally detached from the value produced by that 'thing'. Each new wave of disruption starts as a rebellion against the system, only for the system to eventually absorb it. Stock market -> Crypto -> Memecoins -> the Trump coin.

Speaking of Trump, he's our first meme President. He's a perfect example of what I mean regarding perception preceding reality. He manufacured an image and sold it. Not just as a Presidential candidate but as a real estate developer & businessman. He understood that the image of a thing is more important than the thing itself. Build the golden tower with your name on it. Take loans out. Go into crippling debt over it. Because if everyone believes you are the wealthiest man alive, you might as well be. I truly believe that among a slew of other things his second term ushers in, it invites every American to continue to manufacture an image in every aspect of their daily life & business.

I think in many ways this is totally detrimental, it creates a culture where the value of a thing is actually the hype, attention and image it can create rather than anything else. This, I believe, is why meainstream culture feels so stagnant. In search of capital, it destroys the soul of the thing it's building. We're stuck in a cycle where for things to remain genuine to a community, a story, or a place it has to remain untouch by larger institutional forces This is why there's such a disconnect between perceived value & actual value. These things are also really malleable through lying, shock, controversy. And well, memes.

## What is a meme?

I had to look this up. According to Merriam Webster there are two definitions:
"1. : an amusing or interesting item (such as a captioned picture or video) or genre of items that is spread widely online especially through social media"

But the second definition is much more interesting:
"2: an idea, behavior, style, or usage that spreads from person to person within a culture"

This is what I think I'm touching upon when I say I'm tryna meme the economy. It's an embrace of the ridiculousness, the relative nature of our economy. I really believe that by creating perceived value or perceived attention towards something you can actualize that value. It is better to use the state of the economy, then, to meme (the second definition here) something good into being great. If the economy runs on perception, then the smartest move isn't resisting it—it's using it. If meme status can inflate stocks and dictate careers, why not apply that energy to creating something meaningful?

## What does this mean for Wide Open?

I don't know I'll probably have to build a whole series on this but take for example the postcards during PFW, we created the idea of community first, manufactured it by inviting a bunch of artists together and using an easy-to-produce item to bring as many artists into Wide Open as possible. But tangibly, it did create community. For three days in the heart of Fashion Week, the kids who otherwise would not have had access to this sort of platform could come together & build together, sell together. This is what's important. We have to use it to create something meaningful or it is all for naught.
